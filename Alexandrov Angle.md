
```ad-Definition
title:Definition: Alexandrov Angle
Let $M$ be a metric space, $c(t):[0,a]\to M$ and $c'(t'):[0,a']\to M$ be geodesics starting at $x = c(0) = c'(0)$. The alexanderoff angle is the limsup of the [[Euclidean Comparison Triangle|euclidean comparison angles]] as $t,t'\to0$:
$$
\angle_{x}(c,c') = \limsup_{t,t'\to 0} \angle_x(c(t),c(t'))
$$
```
